The Devil King's Favorite Sweets

...As evening fell, I came to a dire realization. This was an emergency.

I summoned my greatest warlords to the audience chamber to aid me in dealing with it.

(Good. They're all here. I only hope it's not too late.)

Hideyoshi: "My lord, you mentioned an emergency. What is this about?"

Mitsunari: "Could it be? Has there been word of another revolt?"

Nobunaga: "No. This is far more serious."

Mitsuhide: "Perhaps there's some nefarious activity going on in the fiefs?"

Masamune: "I doubt it. We would have heard about it if that was the case."

Nobunaga: "Masamune is correct. It is not that either."

(Those things pale in comparison to this.)

Ieyasu: "...Can you just tell us?"

Nobunaga: "MC has stopped speaking to me."

The shocked looks on everyone's faces spoke volumes.

They understood how grave this matter was as well.

Hideyoshi: "Lord Nobunaga... is that the emergency?"

Nobunaga: "Yes."

(This requires immediate action.)

They were all speechless. I couldn't blame them. Even I was unable to hide my worry.

Nobunaga: "I will listen to any suggestions. This standoff must end."

Masamune was the first to speak. Rather, instead of speaking, he laughed.

Masamune: "I knew the lass had moxie! I've never seen anyone have our lord in a vice like that."

Mitsuhide: "I'll admit, she's something."

(Why do I think they're not taking this seriously?)

Nobunaga: "It's no laughing matter. MC hasn't spoken to me in three whole days."

Nobunaga: "This has never happened before."

(I haven't seen her smile once since this began.)

(I'm starting to believe this could prove fatal.)

Ieyasu: "Um, can I go?"

Nobunaga: "Leaving is forbidden."

Mitsunari: "If we are forced to stay, then... let's see."

Crossing his arms, Mitsunari looked at me with a serious expression.

Mitsunari: "You said she hasn't spoken to you in three days. Did anything happen between you two at that time that could have caused this?"

Nobunaga: "I can't think of anything."

Hideyoshi: "...I've got it!"

Hideyoshi, who had been concentrating hard as well, suddenly spoke up.

Hideyoshi: "Lord Nobunaga, I know what caused this."

(What?!)

Nobunaga: "Is that true, Hideyoshi?"

Hideyoshi: "Yes, my lord. As you'll recall, three days ago we held a banquet to honor the vassals."

Hideyoshi: "And MC was there too."

(Of course she was. I hate being away from her.)

Masamune: "Riiiight. I see where Hideyoshi's coming from."

Mitsuhide: "It's all starting to make sense."

(What are they talking about?)

Mitsunari: "You're right, Lord Hideyoshi. In all probability, that has to be it."

Ieyasu: "...Now that I know what this is about, I really, really want to leave."

(What do they all know? Why won't they tell me?)

I was irritated that the matter remained a mystery to me.

Nobunaga: "Hideyoshi. In plain words, explain what is going on?"

Hideyoshi: "It's that group of entertainers we hired, my lord. They're the cause."

(It's still not making sense to me.)

Masamune: "You don't remember how popular you were with those dancing girls? They showered you with affection."

Mitsunari: "And I remember MC frowning from that point on, until the banquet ended."

Ieyasu: "I think I saw her make a fist each time they poured you wine."

Nobunaga: "I still don't understand."

Mitsuhide: "Lord Nobunaga, the dear girl is jealous. She is experiencing a snit."

(Jealous? Why?)

Nobunaga: "MC shouldn't be jealous."

Nobunaga: "I would never take another woman as my partner now that I have her."

Hideyoshi: "Of course, we all know that. And I'm sure MC knows too."

Hideyoshi: "The head can understand something, but that doesn't mean the heart will go along."

(Is that so?)

Nobunaga: "Now that I know, I will go and talk to MC."

Ieyasu: "...Can we hear what you plan to tell her, first?"

Nobunaga: "'Jealousy is a needless waste of time. Now cease this foolishness!' That is what I am going to say."

Hideyoshi: "M-my lord, pardon me for saying so, but that would probably make matters worse."

(He sounds very certain about that.)

Masamune: "Get her a gift. That'll cheer her up."

Nobunaga: "A gift. I see."

Hideyoshi: "Don't get her anything too fancy! She'll think you've done something really bad and are feeling guilty about it."

Mitsuhide: "Excellent advice, Hideyoshi. Is that how you've kept your popularity with the ladies?"

Hideyoshi: "Mitsuhide, that compliment, if it was a compliment, made me feel a little ill."

Mitsunari: "I believe even a small gift, as long as it came from you, would be meaningful to her."

Ieyasu: "Get her candy. I bet she'll fall all over you if you get her something sweet."

(I see. Candy to sweeten her mood. That makes sense.)

Nobunaga: "Then that is what I shall do."

At once, my men instantly looked relieved.

Nobunaga: "Thank you, everyone. You've been of great service to me."

(Now, I need to prepare.)

Fueled with determination, I stood and left the audience chamber behind.

Once Nobunaga was out of sight, the remaining men exchanged haggard looks.

Mitsuhide: "That was our lord, right? The same man who can lead a charge into the enemy camp without batting an eye?"

Ieyasu: "I think the real emergency is figuring out if there's something in the sake. And if the rest of you are next."

Masamune: "I like seeing him show a couple of flaws."

Mitsunari: "I do too. It may be discorteous of me to say, but I'm actually relieved."

Hideyoshi: "To be honest, me too."

Hideyoshi: "It took a woman like MC to get Lord Nobunaga to finally panic."

The men silently acknowledged that being part of Nobunaga's emotional support group was now one of their duties.

Though none of them, or at least few, found this particularly disagreeable.

......

(Good. MC is still awake.)

Returning to the tenshu, I smiled when I saw her.

She was sitting beside the lamp, sewing. She looked deep in concentration.

Nobunaga: "I've returned."

MC: "Welcome ba--"

(Could it be?)

But MC had cut herself off the second she saw me.

She clicked her tongue and looked the other direction.

(She's still in quite a bad mood.)

(But that ends tonight.)

I approached MC and sat down before her. She still refused to look at me.

Nobunaga: "MC, here."

I opened an ornate glass box and produced a small, colorful sugar candy.

She frowned at it, but I could tell there was a glimmer of curiosity in her eyes.

I offered her the candy. Finally, she took it and placed it in her mouth.

MC's frown melted even as she crunched down on the treat.

MC: "...That's candy. That's real candy!"

(She said something!)

The ache that had settled in my heart these last three days lifted at those words.

Nobunaga: "Yes. It's a gift for you, MC."

MC: "A gift, huh?"

I offered her the box, filled with sugar candies, that I had purchased from a foreign trader.

MC looked at me with a calculating expression.

MC: "Why are you giving me a gift? You did something really bad, didn't you?"

(Hideyoshi's advice was right on the mark!)

(I'll commend him later.)

But to my horror, MC's expression grew darker.

MC: "No answer? Feeling guilty, huh?"

Nobunaga: "I didn't do anything I feel guilty about. At least, I didn't do whatever it is you think I did."

(I only have eyes for you. Always.)

Nobunaga: "I'm giving you the candy so that I can see you smile. I can't be happy when you're unhappy."

Nobunaga: "When you're sad, my heart hurts."

MC: "Nobunaga, you--"

(I believe she told me something similar in the past.)

......

MC: "Are people not allowed to worry about you? Maybe I should send a memo to Hideyoshi!"

Nobunaga: "I don't see why YOU worry about me."

MC: "Do you want me to go down the list?"

MC: "You saved my life, what, four times now?"

MC: "You know Nobunaga, this may surprise you but I'm happy when you're happy."

MC: "And when you're sad, I'm sad. So what I'm saying is--"

MC: "It's my choice if I want to be worried about you!"

......

(Back then, I didn't know what she was talking about.)

(I dismissed what she said, wondering why it would even concern her.)

(But I understand now. That feeling can't be ignored--it's overwhelming.)

MC's pain became my pain. Her joy became mine too.

It wasn't a conscious thought. It was internal, physical.

Nobunaga: "Were you not talking to me because you were jealous?"

MC: "The truth is... yes. I was jealous!"

MC looked down, blushing.

(So it's true.)

Nobunaga: "Mitsuhide called it accurately."

MC: "Uh, you talked to Mitsuhide about this?"

Nobunaga: "Not just him. I was going to tell you jealousy was pointless, but Hideyoshi stopped me."

Nobunaga: "Masamune advised me to get you a gift."

Nobunaga: "Mitsunari suggested something small, and Ieyasu something sweet."

MC: "Wait wait wait wait."

MC: "You told EVERYONE about this?"

Nobunaga: "Yes. I gathered everyone together to discuss how to improve your mood."

MC: "I can't believe it."

(They provided me with an excellent solution. I will definitely seek out their advice in the future.)

I crossed my arms in satisfaction, but MC buried her face in her hands.

MC: "I can't even imagine what they're all thinking right now."

(She's upset again!)

But MC laughed. The sound of it was so sweet it nearly broke my heart.

Her cheerful voice tinkled like a bell.

(Finally, I can hear her laugh again.)

(...She's laughing a lot.)

Nobunaga: "Why are you still laughing?"

MC: "Because you called a WAR COUNCIL after I gave you the silent treatment for a few days! It's just so ridiculous, and--"

(What's so strange about that? I keep them for their wise counsil. Well, no, she may have a point.)

(Perhaps the surprising things she says have more truth in them than I once suspected.)

MC: "I really didn't expect you to get me candy."

Nobunaga: "It was a poor choice?"

MC: "Not at all! It's delicious! I didn't even know you could get candy."

MC: "I don't suppose you know about chocolate?"

(I will have to see about that. As for the candy, I'm pleased, because--)

Nobunaga: "To be honest, I got them for you because I like them too."

Smiling, she held the box out to me.

I took a candy and popped it into my mouth.

The flavor of the sugar rolled over my tongue. I crunched the tiny, spiky drop in my teeth.

Nobunaga: "These are my favorite. I'm very fond of them."

MC: "Okay, that's so cute!"

Nobunaga: "I don't normally like my food sweet, but I often crave these."

MC: "Your reputation would suggest the opposite!"

Nobunaga: "I know. And that's why I only indulge in private."

(The sight of me eating sugar candy shocks people. And somehow, that ruins it for me.)

MC: "The thought of you saving up little treats to eat in your room--!"

MC laughed again, looking happy, and took another candy.

(She's back to herself.)

Nobunaga: "You're smiling again."

Nobunaga: "I'm glad this is something we can enjoy together."

Our gazes locked and I moved closer to her. We shared another treat.

MC gave me a warm look.

MC: "Can I say, I love that you have a sweet tooth?"

(Is that why she's smiling?)

MC: "You have this secret adorable side that I can't resist."

Nobunaga: "MC--"

MC: "I know people say a lot of things about you, but the truth is you're just a cutie."

(A... cutie?)

Nobunaga: "I don't feel that's an apt description at all."

Nobunaga: "You're the cutie."

MC: "Nope, sorry. The real cutie is absolutely my teddy bear and candy-loving Devil King."

The smile MC showed me was sweeter than any candy.

(I'm not sure I see her logic, but maybe that's not important.)

(MC is happy again.)

Relieved, I reached to stroke her cheek. She leaned into my touch.

Then she flung herself against me, as though she had missed me as much as [I] missed her.

She cupped my face in her hands and raised her eyes to mine with a sympathetic look.

(Hmm?)

MC: "Nobunaga?"

MC spoke my name softly. Her face still rested warm against my chest.

MC: "Three days of silent treatment was too much for you. I'm sorry."

(It was!)

Nobunaga: "You've apologized. That's enough. It eases the torment of not having seen your smile for three whole days."

MC: "Okay. But you aren't allowed to let other girls get touchy-feely with you from now on."

Nobunaga: "What?"

MC: "You didn't notice the way they were hanging on you, refilling your cup, and trying to get you drunk?"

MC: "I thought they were cocktail waitresses, not dancers! Anyway, it just didn't feel good."

MC: "You're my boyfriend. It's even an official title!"

(MC--)

(Though I don't know what a cocktail waitress is, I'm strangely pleased to know she got jealous over me.)

With a surge of love for her, I pulled her closer.

Nobunaga: "I apologize too. And I swear to you I'll never let anyone other than you touch me."

Nobunaga: "But you really had nothing to be jealous about."

I tilted her chin up to look into my eyes.

Her eyes were dark with desire, and seeing them stirred my own passion.

Nobunaga: "I didn't even notice them. Because I only have eyes for you."

Nobunaga: "In all the world, I love you alone."

MC: "I love you too."

With those words, I felt truly forgiven.

(She's sweet.)

(So much sweeter than even sugar.)

MC's smile, her voice, her body, her heart--

They were the true indulgences.

Faster than I could voice my desire, I was kissing her.

She kissed me back, a quiet sound of pleasure escaping her lips.

Catching that sound to keep in my heart, to treasure, I held her tight.

For the first time in my life, I knew how sweet jealousy could taste, and how fulfilling it was to make up afterward.

And I was looking forward to sharing many more "firsts" with her.

(I'll never run out of things to love about her.)

(I used to think there was nothing permanent in this chaotic world.)

(But I discovered a small piece of sweet eternity.)

(My feelings for you.)

We went to the floor together, sharing each other's warmth and love. This was happiness.

Yes, there were interruptions, but it was always here, waiting. Our kisses, one after another, showed me that truth.
