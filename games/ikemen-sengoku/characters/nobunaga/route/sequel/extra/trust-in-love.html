Rebelling Against Fate

MC: "Nobunaga...! The brige! It's--"

Nobunaga: "Yes, I can see."

Kicho's explosive device had set the bridge aflame. I watched as the fire spread.

(You always were one to prepare...)

Ieyasu: "Nobunaga! Are you all right?!"

Masamune: "...What the...?"

Masamune and Ieyasu had arrived with their troops; they took in the sight.

(The Seta-no-Karahashi bridge will take three days minimum to repair.)

(Kicho would know that. Meaning he is prepared to go to war in those three days.)

Nobunaga: "It was indeed Kicho that MC met."

Nobunaga: "He was the lone mastermind behind the proliferation of wweapons and the resulting revolts."

Ieyasu: "...Was destroying the bridge his hat trick?"

Masamune: "That was a nice piece of architecture he wrecked."

Nobunaga: "We know his goals now. We must return to Azuchi and prepare for war."

Ieyasu: "...War? What, with Sakai? Because wasn't that where we were planning on going?"

Nobunaga: "It was."

I joined them to briefly explain the current situation. They snapped to attention.

(How quickly they respond at the very mention of the word "war".)

I found it useful. But it reminded me of Kicho's words. How much had I made war and violence a part of my daily life?

......

Kicho: "You are an endless font of hatred and ill will, Nobunaga."

Kicho: "For all that you attempt to achieve unification through violence, the crimes you carry will beget more violence."

Kicho: "...Because you are the spark that keeps the flames of war burning."

Kicho: "As long as you remain alive, the world you are seeking will never come to pass."

Kicho: "Thus, in punishment for your crimes, you will spend your life doing just that."

Kicho: "You will live and keep this country engulfed in war."

......

(Peace will never come as long as I live?)

(My enemies have spat as much and worse in my face, and it never once bothered me.)

(...Perhaps it was my destiny to die in the fires of Honno-ji that night.)

(But I refuse to believe that MC saving my life could destroy this world and my ambitions.)

After all, she had brought too much positive change into my life for that. I looked at her now.

(...She's frowning?)

My heart was in a vice when MC looked that way.

(Why do you make that expression?)

(Because of what Kicho said? No...)

(...It recalls to mind the way you looked when we dealt with the rebellions.)

......

Nobunaga: "Were you frightened?"

MC: "No..."

......

(...And that was because... of me.)

That I should cause her such sadness left me feeling short of breath.

(Do not look that way. I do not want you to suffer when I am near.)

Nobunaga: "MC."

She looked up at me in surprise.

Nobunaga: "If we take what Kicho told us, as long as I can kill him and achieve my goal--"

Nobunaga: "...your future will arrive as it should, and you will continue to live."

Nobunaga: "Nothing has changed."

That did nothing to clear the concern from her expression.

(...Ah, yes. She reacts better to challenges than to reassurance.)

Nobunaga: "Or do you think I amd the kind of man to lose to Kicho's controlling whims?"

MC: "There's no question of that..."

MC: "...You'll definitely do it."

A bit of light returned to her eyes.

(There.)

Nobunaga: "Good."

I took MC's hand in mine and drew her into my arms.

(MC...)

The tighter I held her, the more the pain in my heart and throat grew; and the more I thought back on Kicho's words.

(It is nonsense to say that my life alone will cause more wars to exist.)

(But--)

......

Kicho: "Speaking of victims, the woman you love will die, but she was doomed the moment she was born in that future."

......

(If I cannot bring the wars to a close and complete my unification, MC will remain at risk.)

(I cannot yet see how the two are related, but I know they are.)

The writing disappearing from her book, and her unexplained four day coma...

I relied on logic where others trusted in superstition. But too many otherwise unexplainable events had been occurring.

The thorns inside dug deeper.

(I will not let anyone or anything take MC from me.)

(It is unforgiveable that such warmth should leave this realm forever!)

(I swore to protect her from heaven, earth, and the oceans--whatever the cause of her problems.)

(I would tear down anything that threatened her!)

(...But...)

(If... I am what threatens her life...?)

The dark hole my thoughts descended into had no bottom.

Nobunaga: "...For the moment, you should be your own primary concern..."

Nobunaga: "I will not have you disappear on me before I complete my unification..."

I felt as if my very insides were leaving me when I opened my mouth to speak.

(But it should not matter if my continued existence brings threat to your life.)

(...It is this period of war. As long as I can end that while your health remains, that will solve everything.)

I held MC tighter. She looked up at me.

Nobunaga: "...What?"

MC: "You sound a little... afraid."

(Afraid?)

I had not considered my own emotions until that moment.

(Me... afraid?)

(I do not fear that I might fail to unite these lands!)

(I do not fear that Kicho will stop me...)

(...However... the thought that something might happen to you is...)

The earth below my feet seemed to tilt.

(I have done whatever necessary, taken on any amount of sin to achieve my ambition.)

(Never have I faltered. Never... until the life of this one woman came into question.)

There was nothing puzzling about that.

(It is natural I should feel this way.)

(...Because MC is more important to me than my own life; than the fate of this world.)

(Because I love her.)

I sought to rid myself of the fear she saw; or at least disguise it.

Nobunaga: "Are you taunting me?"

MC: "Wha? No! No, I wasn't!"

Nobunaga: "How very bold. And very foolish. You do know what this means, right?"

MC: "B-but Nobunaga!"

MC protested, turning around in my arms, her gaze darting around us--

To those inspecting the damage to the bridge, and those readying to convey the message of oncoming war.

...Her protests may have had a point.

(Still, I cannot show fear.)

MC: "There's people here... and the bridge is on fire, which is probably more important."

Nobunaga: "We have too few with us to put it out in time. Thus, I deem us free to do as we like while it burns."

Nobunaga: "If you truly wish me to be less free with your body, you should not give up your games of Go so easily."

Laughing, I stroked the curve of her ear and watched as she blushed under my touch.

MC's lashes fluttered as she cast her gaze down; she was so beautiful.

(...How much time do I have?)

(How much time to save you? ...How much time before you disappear forever...?)

There was no answer.

My question disappeared into the dark pit that had come to reside in my heart.
