Happiness to the Kind Demon

It had rained for several days; then, one day, when the sky was finally clear--

(...As always, there is no controlling them.)

My inner circle, that is.

The pandemonium that had been our councils in Azuchi looked to continue in Osaka castle.

Hideyoshi: "All right, cool it! You're in front of Lord Nobunaga, remember! ...Hrgh. Why is it always like this?"

MC: "Aww, I like it when everyone's having fun!"

Mitsuhide: "Oh? Challenging both Hideyoshi and our lord. How bold our little seamstress has become."

MC: "That's bold? No way! I've seen how you guys act! Trust me, no one holds back an opinion in this castle."

MC: "Right, Nobunaga?"

Nobunaga: "Hmm."

MC was laughing and smiling as she talked and joked with them.

I, meanwhile, felt a strange, warm tickle in my chest. Not painful. Just an annoyance.

(...There it is again.)

(It's that tightness in my throat.)

(Am I coming down with something? It does not feel like a cold or cough...)

MC: "Nobunaga? Are you okay?"

Nobunaga: "...Yes."

(And now it has gone away.)

???: "Why is your castle always so noisy?"

A most spiteful voice heralded the entrance of another noisy group.

Nobunaga: "You've arrived."

Kenshin: "Yes. To talk to you. Not your... circus."

Shingen: "I'd describe it more like a dump. Except for the lone, beautiful flower that makes it all worthwhile. Hello again, MC."

Yukimura: "My lord, do you want Nobunaga to fight you? Actually, I know the answer to that."

Yoshimoto: "Is Shingen being quarrelsome? I thought he was just flirting with MC."

Sasuke: "That would be where the problem lies."

After that, the noise lessened for the moment.

Though rising tension took its place.

Masamune: "Small room to hold as many big shots as we've got in here."

Kanetsugu: "The Date clan head can speak when my lord has business with your territory."

Masamune: "I'd say being allies with your ally, meaning you, by extension, gives me plenty of reason to talk--"

Nobunaga: "Enough."

Silence fell over the tenshu once more, though the glares remained.

(The wars have ended, but the aggression and bitterness remain.)

(How much longer until they realize this petty infighting will only damage the value of our alliance?)

Some positive results, perhaps, would convince them that this new union was the right way to do things.

(Something that will satisfy them both...)

I was about to order my own warlords out for the moment when MC leaned over toward me.

MC: "Nobunaga?"

Nobunaga: "What is it?"

MC: "I have a pretty good idea at how to make everyone get along? Are you cool if I try it?"

Nobunaga: "...You have an idea?"

She only smiled slyly in response.

(No one surprises me better than she.)

(...And no one else gets to the heart of a matter as fast as MC either.)

(Very well. I shall have faith in her idea.)

Nobunaga: "You may go ahead."

...What she suggested to everyone was---

......

Keiji: "Alley-oop!"

Shingen: "Not bad, Keiji! Not as good as mine!"

Ieyasu: "...Is it fair that all the stupidly tall and athletic guys were put on the same team?"

Yoshimoto: "I believe that's what they call the luck of the draw--though it is unluck, for others."

Masamune: "Yo, Kanetsugu! You maybe wanna help your teammates on the red team a little?"

Kanetsugu: "Lord Kenshin is on the white team. Why would I help the red team?"

I watched as the Oda warlords, along with the Uesugi and Takeda, tossed some small bags into one of two baskets.

Nobunaga: "What is this game?"

MC: "Oh, just your classic bean bag toss! I had to use a different filler in place of the beans though!"

(I've heard of neither "bean bags" nor a game where you toss them.)

Nobunaga: "And this will put them at peace?"

MC: "Not by the end of round one, no."

MC: "But if they keep playing these fun games with each other? Yeah, I think they'll come to like each other."

Nobunaga: "Is this fun? It appears to be a competition."

Nobunaga: "I cannot see how competing breeds positive emotions toward one another."

MC: "...Yeah? Talk to me again at the end of the game!"

MC grinned; I decided to do as she said and hold my questions.

(There is something positive gained when you win a war or revitalize a town. Even hawking has proven "fun".)

(That is because by the end of it, you have accomplished a goal.)

(But they are even fishing the bags out of the baskets at regular intervals. This game has vague goals and little point.)

Regardless, I watched intently, hoping to understand MC's meaning.

Just then--

Kenshin: "I will finish this!"

Sasuke: "...Lord Kenshin, are you about to invent the mythical triple fast ball?!"

Kenshin had several of the small bags in one hand and pitched them at the basket.

Yukimura: "Watch it! You'll knock it over!"

Ieyasu: "...MC, it's headed your way!"

MC: "What is--?!"

The bags impacted with the basket and knocked it off the high holding platform, sending it flying toward MC.

MC: "Ack!"

(You are a danger to yourself sometimes.)

I grabbed her in my arms and took the blow from the rogue basket to my back.

...It did not hurt. However--

MC: "Whoa, whoaaa?!"

MC, surprised by the whole affair, tumbled backward.

...While gripping me tightly.

That, I was not ready for.

(A danger to both of us--!)

Somehow, I thrust my hands out toward the ground so as to not fall on her.

But the two of us did fall.

Nobunaga: "Oof..."

I looked at MC below me--

...Through the wicker basket which had somehow landed atop my head.

Nobunaga: "..."

I heard two brief gasps, from Hideyoshi and Mitsuhide, if I had to assume.

After that, silence.

Hideyoshi: "Look what your lord did to my lord!"

Kanetsugu: "The fault lies in the shoddy preparation given to this game--which was your side's suggestion, I hasten to point out."

Mitsunari: "I do believe the cause can be traced directly to Kenshin's 'mythical triple fast ball', as Master Sasuke described."

MC: "Sorry! Sorry! This really is all my fault! Are you okay, Nobunaga?"

(Am I... "okay"...?)

I looked around at all the others, still making noise.

I looked down at MC, blushing sheepishly below me.

I looked at the dozens of "bean bags" that had fallen around me. One slipped off my head, dropping the basket lower.

(What is going on...?)

I felt that strange tickle in my chest again. It built.

As it climbed up my throat, I felt as if I had a sudden need to cough.

(Is this...?)

And so I did cough.

Except that it was not a cough that left my lips, but a sudden puff of air--

Nobunaga: "Hmph... Hmph... Ha...!"

MC: "...Nobunaga? Did you hit your head? Can you speak?"

Nobunaga: "Ha ha... Ha ha ha ha ha! Hahaha!"

(There IS no point to this game of hers! It is sheer chaos!)

(My ambition was to quell the chaos in this land. If so, why am I laughing now?)

Mitsunari: "Lord... Nobunaga?"

Yukimura: "Oh yeah. He DEFINITELY hit his head."

I was laughing. When was it that I last laughed this hard? This naturally?

When the impulse left me at last, I looked back down at MC.

Nobunaga: "Haha haaa... Ah. MC."

MC: "Y-yes?"

Nobunaga: "I finally understand."

MC: "Oh!"

(I understand what makes this fun.)

(I understand why experiences like these... why positive experiences build kinship among even former rivals.)

MC's eyes lit up.

MC: "I'm so glad!"

She smiled like a flower in bloom, and I smiled back at her to see it.

At last, I took the basket off my head and stood up.

Nobunaga: "Now then, everyone here will pay for the crime of hitting me with this basket."

MC: "Err! Nobunaga?!"

Nobunaga: "Kenshin Uesugi. It was your strike that knocked it over."

Kenshin: "That's right."

Nobunaga: "You are on the white team, so I will join the red team. And I will score double the amount your entire team has!"

Kenshin: "...Just the fight I've been waiting for!"

I would show them what serious competition was like!

......

MC: "Haha!"

Nobunaga: "You are still laughing."

MC: "Because you got so into the game after that! Watching you was funny!"

MC: "It's been a busy few months, so having that time to relax and goof off felt really great."

Nobunaga: "Yes, it did."

Nobunaga: "Today was... fun."

MC: "Oh yeah?"

Her eyes lit up once more; MC leaned in to kiss me.

(This is my reward for having fun, is it?)

MC: "...Mnm! I'm glad..."

I flicked her tongue with mine, noting how her cheeks took on heat when I pulled away. And I laughed again.

Nobunaga: "Heh."

MC: "And why are you laughing?"

Nobunaga: "Because I love you."

Nobunaga: "And I love the time we spend together. I love it so much I fear as if I have no choice but to laugh."

Nobunaga: "I see you are laughing too now."

MC: "...Ahaha!"

I did not need to hear her response to know we were laughing for the same reason.

It is true there is something positive gained when you achieve a goal.

And there is something positive to be gained in doing nothing at all.

(You have changed my entire worldview.)

(And you do it as if it were nothing at all.)

MC revitalized the world around me, showed me something new and wonderful every time I opened my eyes.

When I balked at the brightness of the sights she showed me, she would just smile and gently show me them again.

MC: "I love you, Nobunaga."

Nobunaga: "I, too, love you."

Someday, we would reminisce on this day.

And when that time came, we would say to each other, "We had fun."

The image of that day I painted in my mind was another dazzling and warm sight that caused me to laugh once more...
