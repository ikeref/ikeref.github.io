Sasuke: "...I told him about the future."

I couldn't believe that Sasuke gave Nobunaga spoilers when he warned me not to! On the other hand--

...The smartest man I knew from the present giving a history lecture to the smartest man in this time period?

(All to save me?)

(What is he thinking of changing--?)

All the sudden, the dark imaginings I'd been having this week coalesced into one really, really bad idea.

Sparked by Kicho's words...

......

Kicho: "Your survival at Honno-ji was nothing short of a miracle."

Kicho: "I fully believe you are the key to destroying the future."

Kicho: "...Because you are the spark that keeps the flames of war burning."

......

Kicho: "Thus, the future you were born in will cease to exist."

Kicho: "You'll die alongside your miserable world."

......

(Kicho implied Nobunaga's survival was the reason the Sengoku would continue.)

(And if it did, history would change and I would die...)

In other words, to bring about peace and an end to the war, Nobunaga needed to die.

That was, in fact, how my timeline had come about. Unification following Nobunaga's death.

(Then what if Nobunaga went to Sasuke to confirm if that was true?)

My blood ran cold.

(He couldn't really be thinking--?)

(Except that Nobunaga was already prepared to sacrifice his heart and soul to achieveing his goals.)

(But isn't that all the more reason he wouldn't give up all he's done and pass things off to another... for my sake?)

(No, right...? He wouldn't! I can just imagine him saying, "Where is the point in such unsubstantiated, foolish--")

That one word triggered a memory.

"...No matter what foolish and dangerous thing I will do to save you..."

MC: "Oh, god..."

The sad look in his eyes when I awoke.

His forcing me to stay here via the bet.

It all clicked together. And I hated it.

(...He's planning on dying?)

Suddenly, my mind was clear.

MC: "Sasuke, do you think you could sneak me out and get me to the battlefield?"

Sasuke: "...You truly wish to go?"

MC: "Yeah. I have to see Nobunaga and... confirm something with him."

MC: "I'm kind of under guard right now, so if I do break out, they're going to chase us. Well? Does that make this a no go?"

Sasuke: "Not at all. Whether it's Azuchi Castle or World 8-4, I'm confident in my princess rescuing abilities."

He stated that impassively but quickly, which filled me with confidence.

MC: "I'm impressed!"

Sasuke: "I am a moderately awesome ninja you know."

Part 7

...It was the day after the Oda left Azuchi.

While wary of surprise attacks on the way to battle, the army stopped at a small lake to rest the soldiers and the horses.

Nobunaga: "Mitsuhide, from this point on, you will proceed according to our plan."

Mitsuhide: "My lord... Good luck in battle."

Mitsuhide bowed once and then exited into the shadows beyond the tent wall.

Keiji arrived after him.

Keiji: "More secret missions for Mitsuhide?"

Nobunaga: "I assign work to those befitting. I am looking forward to your performance in tomorrow's battle."

Keiji: "Good, cause I won't let you down! You want a fighter? Keiji Maeda won't disappoint!"

Keiji: "Though if I could ask one thing of you--"

Nobunaga: "...It is about MC, yes?"

Keiji: "Ah--!"

Nobunaga: "I am aware of your fondness for her."

Keiji: "It's, uh..."

Keiji scratched his head before finally looking up to meet his lord.

Keiji: "...Why'd you leave her behind?"

Nobunaga: "Why is that information important?"

Keiji: "...Only cause it made her sad."

Nobunaga: "Her emotions are her own problem."

Keiji's mouth shot open--

However, no words came out. Despite the cold cruelty of what Nobunaga had said--

...Something about the calmness in his eyes hushed Keiji's complaints.

Nobunaga: "We cannot control others' minds. We can hardly control our own."

Nobunaga: "but we can protect. And I will protect her."

Keiji: "...Hey, uh--"

Keiji: "Sorry for, ya know, stepping out of line."

Nobunaga: "You are forgiven."

Nobunaga: "Take your leave now and relax. I will need that brave spirit come the dawn."

Nobunaga turned and walked out, his coat flowing behind him. Keiji watched him go.

Keiji: "Yo, everyone! Gettin' enough rest?"

Soldier 1: "Haha, we were until you showed up, Keiji! Just kidding! Lord Nobunaga gave us wine tonight so we're doing great!"

Soldier 2: "We've got the biggest battle of our lives looming and our lord is as dignified and majestic as ever!"

Soldier 3: "Well, we only got this far because Lord Nobunaga's been leading us! Which is why we're gonna win this time too!"

Soldier 4: "Yeah, we gotta impress him! Maybe win some prestige for ourselves, too?"

Keiji: "Just don't let your ambition go without your weapons or your head may go rolling off without your body!"

Soldier 1: "True enough!"

Amidst the exuberant laughter of the young soldiers, Keiji heard a rough sounding cough. He followed its source.

Keiji: "You hangin' in there, old man?"

Old Soldier: "Ah, yes. Thank you."

The old soldier thanked Keiji for the bamboo flask of water he offered.

Old Soldier: "I agree that there's something special about Lord Nobunaga..."

Soldier 1: "You think? If an old vet like you says he's the best, that's a real compliment!"

Old Soldier: "But you will know the true strength of a lord like him when he is gone."

Keiji: "What do you mean, gone?"

Old Soldier: "The sudden loss of a leader both respected and admired by all his troops--"

Old Soldier: "...There is a rage and a grief there strong enough to overcome any powerful force."

Old Soldier: "Their morale unbound by the living, his soldiers would bring literal hell in their lord's name."

The old soldier spoke softly, reminiscent.

Keiji: "But... he wouldn't..."

Soldier 1: "Yeah, what's with the depressing talk, gramps? Don't get us worried, now!"

Soldier 2: "You've already got one foot in the grave yourself!"

Soldier 3: "That's pretty funny!"

Soldiers: "Hahaha!"

Masamune: "You and the lads getting pumped?"

Keiji: "Hey, Masamune. Thirsty?"

Masamune: "Yeah, get me a cup of the good stuff!"

Keiji: "...Seriously? Someone's ready to relax--and by relax, I mean snooze!"

Masamune: "I'm joking with you, lad. Obviously."

Masamune: "Unlike the rest of you! Think it's a good ideea to bet on Lord Nobunaga's death within a league of his hearing?"

Soldier 1: "We weren't really! It was all just talk!"

Masamune: "...As long as that's all it is."

Masamune: "If you want to talk about a scary fight, a dead hero coming back to life can do a lot for morale too. On that note--"

Masamune: "Motonari Mouri's return to the field's got to be a big shot of moxie for his troops. Winning won't be easy."

Masamune: "Feel free to unwind but stay sharp."

Part 8

Masamune: "Feel free to unwind but stay sharp."

With that warning given, Masamune left.

Keiji: "...The death and return of a hero..."

Soldier 1: "Pretty sure guys like us have our hands full just keeping ourselves alive!"

Keiji: "C'mon now, I don't wanna hear my vanguard talking like that! You wouldn't let me charge on my lonesome now, will you?"

Several more days passed.

The mountains surrounding the plain that would soon host a great battle were dotted with men and horses.

Hideyoshi: "My lord."

Mitsunari: "Our enemy have been cautious in setting up their encampments."

Ieyasu: "We've got the Hojo coming from the northeast, and the Miyoshi and Chosokabe coming from Shikoku."

Mitsunari: "The Uesugi and Takeda are camped in those mountains over there."

Hideyoshi: "...That's far from the battle. What's their aim? Are they trying to pincer us?"

Keiji: "In any case, we know they won't be making the first move."

Everyone from the warlords to the lowest ranked soldiers knew that this would be the battle to decide everything.

Nobunaga: "We will win this war."

Tension and heated emotion filled the area.

True to his word, Sasuke had no problems getting me out of Azuchi Castle. We boosted a horse and... were now resting.

MC: "I'm sorry we keep having to stop for me."

Sasuke: "This is far from an unnecessary amount of stopping. In fact, I'm impressed at the amount of travel you can fit in a day."

(Not as much as Nobunaga can. We haven't seen a trace of him yet...)

  • (I can't rest though.)
  • (I want to see him soon.)
  • (I need him to be safe.)

MC: "I'm rested though, so if you're ready I think we should get back on the road."

(I want to talk to Nobunaga before the battle gets too heated to see him.)

(If we get there before it starts, he'll be in camp. But if he leaves for the front--)

(...Who knows if I'll see him in time?)

MC: "They wouldn't really kill you if--"

MC: "Hmm?"

(Bandits? Or soldiers?)

(Either way, we can't let them find us!)

Sasuke took my hand and was about to lead me into some tall grass to hide--

...But I stopped at the ringing of what was clearly a prayer staff.

(That sound!)

Sasuke: "MC!"

Kennyo: "What cause does the Devil King's lover have on an animal trail this far from home?"

Part 9

Kennyo: "What cause does the Devil King's lover have on an animal trail this far from home?"

MC: "...Kennyo."

He and his men formed a circle around us, cutting off any escape route we had.

MC: "Sorry, Sasuke. I didn't mean to get us surrounded."

Sasuke: "It's all right. I've trained for this. More times than you might imagine."

MC: "Ninja training sounds rough."

Sasuke: "Only when you're trying to pass your license exam from moderately awesome ninja to fully awesome ninja."

(I'm so glad Sasuke can keep his sense of humor in situations like these.)

Kennyo: "I would have thought you would be at the battlefield, at Nobunaga's side."

Kennyo: "Yet here you are, in the wilderness, with only one guard. Has he forsaken you?"

MC: "...Our relationship isn't your business."

Kennyo: "Hmph. You remain impertinent."

Kennyo: "That is fine. If you do not stand in my way, I see no reason not to spare you."

MC: "Oh. About that--"

(I would LOVE to not stand in your way but we happen to be going the same direction.)

(If he gets to Nobunaga first, there's gonna be a fight and I won't get to talk to him!)

(Somehow, we need to distract Kennyo and sneak away...)

I shot Sasuke a quick look.

Sasuke looked in the direction we'd left our horse, then looked back at me.

He did this weird blinking thing too. Code?

(I'm going to assume that means "You distract him, then we escape on horse together!" Great! Good plan, Sasuke!)

I nodded and turned to Kennyo.

(Now to distract him.)

As I searched for ideas, my mind flitted back to that pink sugar candy--and the color pink reminded me of one person.

MC: "...I hear you left Ranmaru behind."

Kennyo: "What? Is it casual conversation you seek?"

MC: "Oh, I'm just wondering what your reason is for abandoning someone who cares so much about you and even treated you."

MC: "You know you can still go back to him."

Kennyo: "Ranmaru is too weak to walk the path I walk. Why should I go back to him?"

("Weak?!" Hey now, hold your adorable pink horses there, buddy!)

My temper flared when I thought of Ranmaru and how he smiled through his heartache.

MC: "Ranmaru is not weak!"

Kennyo: "How do you wish me to describe him? I left him because he is too weak to follow me and thus useless."

There was pain in Kennyo's expression; his callous words were clearly hard-won.

(...You don't really believe that. You're just trying to convince yourself.)

(And you really need to stop poisoning your heart with those lies.)

MC: "You can leave him behind; you can think he's just going to slow you down or even lessen your resolve--"

MC: "...But Ranmaru is doing everything he can to try and bring you happiness."

MC: "Because you make him happy! He doesn't want to lose you, even if he's not strong!"

MC: "How can you just discard someone who only wants you to be happy?"

I had to say, I felt like I was in Ranmaru's position myself. I was so worked up I'd started to cry.

Kennyo: "...What do you know...?"

Kennyo: "You cannot possibly have experienced the kind of hatred that burns your soul every waking moment of your life."

I might not know hatred, but I felt rage.

MC: "Okay, you know what you are? Stubborn, that's what! A big, stubborn oaf of a man!"

Kennyo: "...S-stubborn?"

Sasuke: "MC, he's also an armed oaf--no offense meant by the O word, sir--and maybe you should be more careful?"

MC: "No, I think he needs to hear this!"

Sasuke: "And I think we've stalled long enough."

(Oh, right! I was distracting him! Oops! Got a bit too into my role!)

Sasuke whistled and our horse came rushing over with a neigh.

Brethren 1: "Wha?!"

Brethren 2: "Where were they hiding a horse?!"

Our trusty war steed came crashing into Kennyo's line of men and Sasuke vaulted up upon her with ninja-agility.

He reached down a hand to pull me up.

Sasuke: "MC!"

MC: "Sasuke!"

Before I could take it--

Kennyo: "You will not escape!"
