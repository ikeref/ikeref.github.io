Nobunaga - Sequel - Chapter 1 (1-5)

Part 1

MC: "...What?!"

The next page of my Traeler's Guide was blank.

I flipped through several more blank pages. Some things, like landmarks, advertisements and profiles, remained.

But a huge swath of pages supposedly telling the history of the period were just... empty.

(What does this mean?)

I felt a chill creep up through me.

Wishing it would go away, I shut the Traveler's Guide.

(Something's going to change history. In a massive way.)

(I've got to tell everyone!)

Pushing through the panic that threatened to overtake me, I began to race toward Azuchi Castle.

It was mostly empty of people. I took the opportunity to assess the damage.

The castle's inner palace is holding up, unlike the tenshu.

Outside of the burnt wreckage looming over the building, you almost couldn't tell it had been under attack.

(I'm worried. I heard everyone survived...)

(...But where was Nobunaga when the tenshu was attacked?)

(Was he hurt? Is it bad? Is he...?)

MC: "...Take it easy, MC..."

But my voice quaked as visions of Nobunaga, injured, plagued my mind.

???: "MC!"

MC: "Is that--?"

Just as I'd begun to run, I heard someone call my name.

Hideyoshi: "You're back!"

MC: "Hideyoshi! And--?"

???: "Hey there, Princess! I've heard the rumors about you."

(...Weird, as I don't know anything about you. Except that you dress like a theater actor who thinks he's still on stage.)

Couldn't fault the man, though, as the colorful clothes suited him. Very well.

MC: "Hi there. Yes, I'm MC. It's nice to meet you. Um--?"

Keiji: "Keiji Maeda's my name, though I answer to 'that eccentric guy over there!' and 'Hey, you!' You can take your pick!"

He had big, shining eyes like a cat's and a smile like a bright sunny day.

Fear and worry still clouded my mind, but I couldn't help but smile with him.

MC: "Okay, eccentric guy."

Keiji: "You've already got the hang of it!"

Hideyoshi: "...You forgot to include 'problem child' in your list of names."

Hideyoshi: "MC, hard as it is to believe, Keiji's also an Oda vassal-state warlord."

Hideyoshi: "He had the gall to LEAVE without saying anything, but he did return with information about the attack."

Hideyoshi: "We had just enough time to inform Ieyasu and Masamune too. They're back as well. Everyone's back."

MC: "Where's Nobunaga though?"

Hideyoshi: "In war council. You look like you'd like the chance to see him for yourself."

I followed Hideyoshi and Keiji into the audience chamber where I saw the familiar faces of the Oda warlords.

(Nobunaga!)

But my eyes went straight to the man I loved, sitting atop the dais. The weight in my heart lifted to see him.

Nobunaga: "MC, welcome back."

Part 2

Nobunaga: "MC, welcome back."

MC: "...Thanks. I'm glad to be back!"

I probably shouldn't have sounded so happy under the circumstances, or smiled so much, but I couldn't help it.

(Good. He doesn't look injured.)

(...Maybe I should check though? No, no! Down, girl! It's war council time!)

Restraining the desire [to] embrace him, I forced myself down in my seat at the back of the room.

Masamune: "Here I thought we were going to see you leap into his arms, lass."

Mitsuhide: "If it helps, you maybe pretend the rest of us aren't here."

  • "Not now."
  • "Gee, thanks, Mitsuhide."
  • "I don't need to pretend."

MC: "Ahem! For your information, I still wouldn't because it's war council time."

Nobunaga: "How admirable."

Nobunaga: "If you are able to restrain yourself after several days without me, I shall have to try harder to break that resistance."

MC: "Yeah, I-- What? ...Is that a threat or a promise, Nobunaga?"

Nobunaga: "I will let you decide which when I am done making love to you."

Ooh, the way he could turn my insides to lava with one teasing [look] from his deep carnelian eyes.

(Maybe I shouldn't have restrained myself in front of everyone after all?)

Mitsuhide: "Now that MC's punishment has been settled upon, let us return to business."

Mitsuhide: "May I continue my report, my lord?"

Nobunaga: "You may."

The joviality of the room fell away at the sound of that voice.

Even I modeled myself after the stern faces around me.

(I'd like to be caught up on our situation as quickly as possible.)

Mitsuhide: "News of the attack has spread throughout the region, just days after the destruction of the tenshu."

Mitsuhide: "I would say there is no one across these islands who does not know."

Mitsuhide: "Which is why, as expected, we have seen a rash of insurrections brewing."

MC: "Our unification shaken because Nobunaga bled just one drop...?"

Masamune: "It was a big drop. Azuchi Castle was a symbol of Lord Nobunaga's authority, and now there's a huge hole through it."

Masamune: "I'm certain the rumors have him already on his last legs."

Masamune: "If his domain is going to go to pieces, then everyone's going to scramble to add bits of it to their own."

Mitsunari: "It was to be expected. What was not is the large number of previously loyal vassals who have turned on us..."

Mitsunari: "Our spies indicate there is a third party riling them up."

Masamune: "Someone's convinced them they can become the next Nobunaga if they're quick enough, huh?"

Hideyoshi: "This isn't a laughing matter, Masamune."

Ieyasu: "Especially if that third party ends up being Kicho."

(Kicho?)

I didn't recognize the name.

(But they sound like they're on a roll, so I'll ask about this Kicho later.)

Hideyoshi: "We don't know that for sure."

Hideyoshi: "Keiji's investigation puts Kicho as being behind the artillery attack..."

Hideyoshi: "...But it's difficult to move when we don't have hard evidence against him."

Keiji: "He's been holed up in his merchant house the entire time."

Keiji: "It'd be a different story if we captured weapons, men, evidence... anything from that night."

Mitsuhide: "Not to mention our mastermind has made no further attempt on our lord's life, nor his territory."

Mitsuhide: "Their motive remains a mystery."

(So, it's likely this Kicho is behind the strike, but we can't pin it on him yet.)

(If it is him, we don't know why he did it nor what he plans to do next.)

Mitsuhide: "Unless instability was their goal and they have already achieved exactly what they wanted."

Mitsuhide: "Regardless, we have two issues here."

Mitsuhide: "Putting down the insurrections, and uncovering the aims of the mastermind behind the attack on the tenshu."

Part 3

Mitsuhide: "Regardless, we have two issues here."

Mitsuhide: "Putting down the insurrections, and uncovering the aims of the mastermind behind the attack on the tenshu."

A thoughtful silence fell over the hall.

I had a feeling it was bad, but we're actually in desperate straits here.

We'd had an insurrection after the Honno-ji incident and my arrival, but this was far more serious.

Not that any insurrection was easy. I recalled Yoshitoshi, who'd tried to poison us and been slaughtered in turn.

......

Nobunaga: "There's a difference between those who've recently joined you, and those who have served you for generations."

Nobunaga: "If someone that close to you stabs you in the back, they will never return to your side."

Nobunaga: "You have to lance a festering wound."

Nobunaga: "Or else disease will eat away at you from within."

......

(I thought those executions were too brutal, and came too swiftly. But I know why he did it...)

......

Nobunaga: "The first time I killed a man, I was thirteen years old."

Nobunaga: "He was an assassin sent by my brother."

Nobunaga: "It's kill or be killed. You only have one choice if you want to live. Thus, there's no reason to hesitate."

Nobunaga: "That's when I swore to gain enough power to put a stop to all the fighting."

......

(Once more, he'll be forced to kill those who once served him. Without mercy.)

I hated the thought of Nobunaga freezing his heart once more to deal with the weight of his sin.

I wished the pain in my own heart could somehow lessen his.

(Though I musn't forget that my being here with him is helping him.)

(I'm here to provide him warmth and help him hold onto his humanity.)

(Remind him of when it's safe.)

(...Nothing can take me from your side, Nobunaga. Nothing.)

A calm, majestic voice broke the storm of silence dominating the hall.

Nobunaga: "I expect not a one of you here..."

Nobunaga: "...is even considering that something as trivial as this will put an end to my campaign of unification."

Hideyoshi: "We are all ready, my lord, to continue carving your path forward."

Mitsuhide: "Those who stand in your way will rue their choice, though not for long."

There wasn't a look of doubt among them.

Keiji: "After all, you've got me back!"

Ieyasu: "...For how long? I hope you know that there's no point in bragging if you just wander off again."

Keiji: "Don't get mired in the past!"

Masamune: "He's right, Ieyasu. You know you're happy to see him again."

Ieyasu: "No, I'm not. See this? Does this look like a happy expression?"

Mitsunari: "I must say, Lord Keiji's return has made everything that much more lively."

It hadn't taken long for the warlords to break into their usual banter. It was calming.

(They recover so fast! I guess that's part and parcel of being famous historical--)

(...Right! The guide book!)

Part 4

(They recover so fast! I guess that's part and parcel of being famous historical--)

(...Right! The guide book!)

That reminded me of my nearly blank copy of "A Traveler's Guide to Japan's Hottest Warlords."

(It's possible the current destabilization has something to do with the disappearing history.)

(That said, hardly any of them know about the magazine.)

(I'd like another opinion on it, but I think it's going to confuse them more than bring light to the situation.)

While I was deciding what to do, council reached its conclusion, and Nobunaga dismissed the others.

Nobunaga: "Hideyoshi, Mitsunari, you are to stay."

Both: "Yes, lord."

Nobunaga: "MC."

Hime: "Yes?"

Nobunaga: "Wait for me in my room in the inner palace."

My heart trembled at the hint of sweetness in his voice.

MC: "...I will."

(Maybe I'll tell him about the guidebook there in private.)

It was Mitsuhide who caught me outside the audience hall and showed me to Nobunaga's new quarters.

MC: "Minus the large, unintended skylight, the castle hasn't changed much."

Mitsuhide: "Not on the surface. There are ripples everywhere, but everyone has faced worse, one way or another."

MC: "I'm glad that we still have a home. Huh. What's going on there?"

In the garden, several soldiers seemed to be engaging Keiji in some practice.

Soldier: "I'm not done yet!"

Keiji: "Yeah, that's the spirit! That stroke right there? You're getting better!"

Keiji: "...Better, but not best!"

Soldier: "Whoa!?"

Soldier 2: "Haha! He knocked you on your ass!"

Keiji: "Who's laughter do I hear? Sounds like someone wants to volunteer!"

Soldier 2: "Ugh! W-wait! I didn't--ack!"

Soldier 3: "You can't say you didn't deserve that!"

Soldier 4: "Good one, Lord Keiji!"

MC: "Huh. They make getting the stuffing knocked out of you look fun."

I couldn't help but smile to see a little joie de vie back in the faces of Azuchi Castle's residents.

Mitsuhide: "Keiji Maeda lacks for neither military skill nor charm."

Mitsuhide: "You won't find a single Oda soldier who doesn't respect him, if not adore him outright."

MC: "It looks like it."

Mitsuhide: "The only question is why does he have such adoration for us?"

MC: "Is there a reason he wouldn't?"

Mitsuhide: "There are many who hate Lord Nobunaga simply because they have heard of his villainous deeds."

Mitsuhide: "Others have a more personal reason. And they are most pesky, as a result."

Mitsuhide: "Some lost their station, some their families, some those they love..."

Mitsuhide: "Keiji's adopted father was evicted from his own land by Lord Nobunaga's own father, leaving them homeless."

MC: "Oh..."

Mitsuhide: "Keiji worked his way back up, serving various lords until becoming a retainer of his uncle--an Oda vassal."

Mitsuhide: "A tale like this would normally end in revenge against the son of the man who took everything from him, however..."

MC: "...Keiji doesn't seem interested in getting revenge, right?"

Still, it astonished me how carefree Keiji looked now, given all the struggles of his childhood.

Mitsuhide: "Certainly not against Lord Nobunaga. That much, I can assure you."

Mitsuhide: "Speaking of, you'll find our lord's room just past here. Enjoy yourself."

MC: "A-ahem. Thank you."

I bowed to Mitsuhide as he left, but I didn't move; his words stuck with me.

(A personal reason...)

(I wonder if whoever is behind this attack feels that Nobunaga took something important from them?)

(But even if you give back what was taken, you can't undo the original loss. How do you erase that hate?)

Just as I found myself sinking into depressive thoughts...

Keiji: "Hey, Princess."

Part 5

Keiji: "Hey, Princess. Not heading straight for Lord Nobunaga's room?"

MC: "Oh, Keiji..."

There was a fun-loving joy to his voice that suited his bright smile.

Keiji: "Judging by the look on your face, someone gave you the long and short on my story."

MC: "I, err, yes?"

(I wasn't looking. Was he watching Mitsuhide and I talking?!)

MC: "I'm sorry. I hope I haven't made things awkward..."

Keiji: "Awkward's always welcome here. And besides, Lord Nobunaga's princess has a right to know my story."

Keiji: "Especially if I might be putting your lord in danger. Right...?"

MC: "I'm not worried about that!"

MC: "...But I am curious about how you came around on him and the Oda."

Keiji: "We're standing on the cusp of history. Being mad about your misfortunes isn't just shortsighted, it doesn't help."

It's like he spoke to my earlier worries.

Keiji: "I didn't like how mopey things got in my family. I decided to lighten things up, by myself, if I had to."

Keiji: "Turns out, smiles are infectious."

(Smiles are infectious, huh?)

Silly as it sounded, it made sense.

And it spoke to the truth of the man behind the flashy dress and big smiles.

(I get it.)

MC: "You pay a lot of attention to what's going on around you."

Keiji: "Hmm?"

MC: "Well, you stopped what you were doing to come talk to me, right as I was feeling down."

MC: "That wasn't a coincidence, I'm guessing."

(He probably didn't hear us, what with his training. Meaning he picked up all that just by observing me.)

Keiji: "...Heck, I'd question the sense of a guy who couldn't see how you were feeling."

Keiji: "I'll be around if you need some cheering up, though."

Keiji: "See ya!"

MC: "...Oof! Haha!"

Keiji gave me a strong pat on the back.

I recovered in time to see him wave as he returned to his garden sparring.

(Smiles are infectious. Smiles are infectious...)

(I should remember that advice for when I'm with Nobunaga.)

I hurried to his new room.

......

Keiji glanced back at MC out of the corner of his eye.

Keiji: "You're the observant one."

Keiji: "...She's an unusual woman."

......

(It shouldn't be long until Nobunaga gets here himself.)

I was so impatient I could barely focus on Nobunaga's new room. Though I did spy something that made me happy.

(Aww! He's already got Bearsace up!)

Next to all the lavish artwork and items of splendor was a fluffy bear with big eyes and an even bigger heart.

I smiled to see he was safe, when--

Nobunaga: "Have I kept you waiting?"

MC: "Not at all!"
