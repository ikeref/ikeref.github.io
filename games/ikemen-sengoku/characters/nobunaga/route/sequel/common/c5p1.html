Nobunaga - Sequel - Chapter 5 (1-5)

Part 1

Ranmaru: "I'll do anything to stop Master Kennyo."

Ranmaru: "But I can't do it alone."

Ranmaru: "I need your help. Please..."

He disappeared quickly after handing over the letter.

I returned inside and processed everything that had just happened.

(Ranmaru wants to keep Kennyo from going through with his revenge and hurting himself further.)

He had his concerns, surely, but Ranmaru looked calm and resolute.

(The ability to put the person you love before all else is amazing.)

(...Would I be able to do the same thing if I were in his position?)

I tucked the letter away.

Just then, the door slid open.

Nobunaga: "You are awake? How are you feeling?"

MC: "My body's still a bit weak, but I took a walk in the garden and was just fine."

I took a quick look at Nobunaga.

(He doesn't seem as miserable as he did yesterday. That's good.)

(Actually, he seems really content.)

(Maybe nothing happened while I was unconscious? Well, nothing I need to worry about, that is.)

(He could have been upset because he was so worried. That makes sense.)

(...What doesn't make sense is him not telling me I'd been out for four days. I should really ask him about that.)

Nobunaga: "Why do you look at me that way? Does something about my face displease you?"

MC: "Definitely not! I think your face is perfect."

I flashed him a brief smile before getting to the issue at hand.

MC: "It's just, you could have told me."

Nobunaga: "Told you what?"

MC: "...About the fact that I was asleep for a whole four days."

Perhaps he was surprised I knew.

MC: "I'm sorry to leave you alone for so long."

Nobunaga said nothing.

The crinkle in his brow deepened though.

(Yeah, that look tells me he was far more than worried.)

In which case, it was my turn to be strong.

(I can't reassure him that it will never happen again, but--)

MC: "The good news is, I'm here now."

Nobunaga was back to looking shocked. Still, it was an upgrade from sad!

MC: "Back to my original concern. Why didn't you let me know I'd been--?"

Nobunaga: "...If you are able to walk, walk with me."

MC: "Hmm?"

Nobunaga: "I wish to see the castle town with you at my side."

Nobunaga: "Come along."

MC: "Hold on--!"

He took my hand and led me out without giving me much choice in the matter.

(Ahh, there goes my question.)

I caught a few more glimpses at his face as we passed through the castle dates. Nothing I saw answered my question.

(...On the other hand, a date with Nobunaga is a surprisingly good deal.)

(I didn't think he'd have time for one what with a big war looming.)

In the end, I tried to be positive about it. Well, I didn't need to try TOO hard. I was genuinely happy about this.

MC: "Where are we going?"

Nobunaga: "I haven't decided."

MC: "So, this is a long walk date?"

Nobunaga: "...I am not familiar with the concept. Is that a common thing in your time?"

MC: "Kind of. You and your boyfriend hold hands and get all cuddly, enjoying each other's company more than the destination!"

Nobunaga: "Then yes, I suppose--"

Nobunaga: "...This is a long walk date."

Part 2

Nobunaga: "Then yes, I suppose--"

Nobunaga: "...This is a long walk date."

Nobunaga seemed so amused to say it.

(Ah... I love it when he adopts modernisms!)

(He's so cute! Oh, speaking of cute--)

(I meant to give Ranmaru's information over to him. I should remember to do that.)

Merchant 1: "Goodness, if it isn't our lord! How can we help you? Something for the war?"

Merchant 2: "We're happy to contribute anything we can for the cause, free of charge!"

Nobunaga: "Then continue your business as usual. That will do our country better than flattery."

Merchant 2: "As you say, my lord!"

Merchant 1: "You make a good point!"

(...But maybe now's not the time.)

I kept an eye on him though, as we caught up with the various merchants and others.

(I always like seeing how the townspeople treat Nobunaga. It shows his policies are working.)

(Even with a nasty war on the horizon, everyone trusts he'll protect them.)

Tea Proprietress: "Princess, it's nice to see you again!"

MC: "Nice to see you too! It's so good to be back on my feet."

Tea Proprietress: "So, that rumor about you being under the weather was true? Poor dear. In any case, I'm glad you seem better."

Tea Proprietress: "What do you say? Would you and the lord like to have some tea and sweets?"

MC: "That sounds perfect!"

Nobunaga: "We shall dine here then."

At my enthusiasm, Nobunaga nodded and put an arm around my shoulder.

MC: "Nobunaga?"

Nobunaga: "You mentioned hand holding earlier. Is holding you this way not acceptable?"

MC: "Maybe a little inconvenient for dining."

Nobunaga: "Is that an invitation to continue once we are done?"

Tea Proprietress: "I see you two are as sweet on each other as always!"

I was already blushing thanks to Nobunaga, but my face grew ten degrees hotter when the proprietress laughed.

Tea Proprietress: "Come on in, now!"

(I suppose it's nice to know we can bring a little joy to the people in these times.)

We sat down on the red cloth benc as tea and sweets were brought to us.

MC: "Those are cute looking!"

The sweets were round and pinwheel like. They had a soft, translucent pink color.

Husband: "We borroed some sugar and hard candy from Lord Nobunaga the other day--"

Husband: "...and with it were able to replicate this new type of crystal sugar candy the Nanban traders spoke of."

Nobunaga: "Your craftsmanship is excellent, comparable to the original."

Nobunaga: "Continue polishing your craft, so that I may see your work presented at some celebratory affair or another."

Husband: "Your words are too much, my liege."

The proprietress's husband bowed to us before returning to the kitchen.

(They're really pretty. That pink, peachy color reminds me of--)

MC: "Ah!"

Nobunaga: "What is it?"

Nobunaga had just tossed one of the treats into his mouth.

MC: "I've got something to give you."

I pulled the letter out of my kimono.

MC: "Ranmaru gave this to me."

Nobunaga: "You met with him?"

Part 3

MC: "Ranmaru gave this to me."

Nobunaga: "You met with him?"

(No need to hide it. Ranmaru may have turned against him for Kennyo's sake, but he's no enemy to Nobunaga now.)

MC: "Yes. When I was walking in the garden."

Nobunaga: "He is talented indeed to be able to infiltrate into the inner palace."

Funny how Nobunaga seemed pleased.

He took the letter and opened it.

(I haven't looked at it myself.)

(But it should have information on Kennyo and how Ranmaru wants to stop him.)

Nobunaga: "...He has finally made up his mind?"

(Nobunaga's smiling again. Huh.)

MC: "What did Ranmaru write?"

Nobunaga: "He wishes to capture Kennyo before he can join the Mouri in battle."

Nobunaga: "And for that, he seeks our assistance."

MC: "Sounds just like what he told me."

MC: "Ranmaru said he thinks we can free Kennyo from his hate without, you know, letting him kill you."

MC: "But to be freed, Kennyo needs to be alive."

MC: "...I hope you can work together to find a way to spare him, please?"

(Ranmaru couldn't bear losing either Nobunaga or Kennyo. They're both important to him.)

Nobunaga: "That depends on how things go."

MC: "...That's... fair."

(If Ranmaru can't get through to Kennyo, Nobunaga may have to stop him by force...)

(...But I hope Ranmaru's plan works!)

If it did... well, that was one less problem to worry about--in a sea of problems.

(So many threats looming. Motonari Mouri, Kenshin Uesugi, Shingen Takeda...)

(I really hope we don't have to count Kennyo among them.)

(I trust in the Oda forces' strength, which is exactly why I'm worried for them. Our enemies are too crafty.)

(And if we have more traitors before the battle? We're shaken up enough as is.)

(...But I won't be shaken! I'll take after Ranmaru and be firm in my resolve.)

I'd no sooner made that decision when--

Nobunaga: "MC, I must tell you something."

MC: "Sure. What is it?"

Nobunaga: "I will not be taking you to battle."

MC: "But--"

(What does he mean, he won't take me?)

(I know it sounds like a weird thing to question after how much I hated it at first, but to quit now?)

MC: "...Why?"

Nobunaga: "Because you are not in perfect condition."

MC: "This is about me being asleep for four days? I'll get my strength back in time for--"

Nobunaga: "I won't repeat myself. You will stay in Azuchi."

MC: "I..."

(I know he's right.)

(This would be a bad time to go, when I could pass out on the battlefield at any moment.)

(At the same time, hearing I can't be with him at this important juncture is just...!)

Nobunaga: "You must... wait patiently for my return."

For a moment, he looked away.

(Hmm?)

That tiny action disturbed me greatly.

(Why did he break his gaze...?)

Nobunaga: "This long walk date is my way of giving you something to recall in my absence."

(...It's like he's hiding something.)

I couldn't pinpoint why I felt that.

But all the worry I'd neatly tucked away came spilling back out with that action.

Suddenly everything became suspicious.

(So, if he is hiding something, then his earlier deflection would be on purpose.)

(And this could all tie back to his strange mood the other night...!)

MC: "...Nobunaga, I challenge you!"

Nobunaga: "You challenge me to what?"

MC: "To a game of Go! Same terms as before, but if I win, you'll let me go with you."

Nobunaga: "...Very well. I accept."

Part 4

MC: "To a game of Go! Same terms as before, but if I win, you'll let me go with you."

Nobunaga: "...Very well. I accept."

......

(I've got to win this.)

(I can't put my finger on the reason why, but I can't let Nobunaga go off on his own.)

The normally soothing clack of the stones was maddening to me now, which was awful because I needed a cool head!

(C'mon, me! Where'd all that training go?!)

Nobunaga kept his cool.

He was also as ruthless as usual.

It was easy to see he had command of the board. Each move precise.

MC: "...Ugh."

There'd been no victory or fun in this match. And now it was over.

MC: "I... lost."

Nobunaga: "So it seems."

He straightened up and looked down at me.

My loss began to weigh on me. It had been a while since the stakes felt this high.

(Can he sense I'm worried about him?)

(Is there some reason beside my health that he won't take me along?)

(...What is it about my presence that would make things inconvenient for him?)

Nobunaga: "Now you will promise aloud you will not leave Azuchi until the war with the Mouri and their allies is over."

MC: "Are you serious?"

Nobunaga: "Yes."

MC: "Nobunaga, this is going to be the biggest war we've faced yet, right?"

MC: "You're really telling me to stay put through the whole thing?"

Nobunaga: "I won. You will say it."

(Not only does he know I'm onto him, he won't tell me anything!)

Nobunaga: "Say you will not leave Azuchi."

MC: "No."

Nobunaga: "You agreed to using our previous terms."

I kept silent.

Nobunaga: "...You are too stubborn. Do you want me to force the words out of you?"

He caught my chin in his long fingers.

(You're going to have to, as I'm not going to give in like this!)

The sick feeling in my heart wouldn't let me.

  • (I'm afraid...)
  • "If you're worried..."
  • "I'm your lucky charm."

MC: "You dragged me along to battles before, even when I begged you not to! And you know what? You protected me."

MC: "I'm your lucky charm, aren't I...?"

Nobunaga: "...You are. And that will not change no matter how far apart we are."

MC: "Just--...!"

Nobunaga: "Do you intend to renege on not just this bet but the previous one?"

MC: "The previous one?"

Nobunaga: "You don't recall? I made you promise you would forgive and allow any dangerous, foolish thing I do to save you."

......

Nobunaga: "...This is what I want you to say..."

Nobunaga: "Say that no matter what foolish and dangerous thing I will do to save you--"

Nobunaga: "...you will forgive and allow it."

MC: "I promise... No matter what foolish thing you do to protect me--"

MC: "I will always... even if it's me alone... I will always forgive you..."

......

MC: "I said that, yes, but that was only--!"

Nobunaga: "Only what?"

Would I really tell him I lost on purpose? That he forced those words out of me?

(I can't look him in the eyes and say that.)

It wasn't true, anyway. I was happy for it then, and I said those words gladly.

(He's really leaving me behind to save me?)

(Am I feeling nervous over nothing?)

Nobunaga: "Are the words we share so meaningless to you that you would take them back?"

MC: "No! I wouldn't... do that."

(And I won't go back on this latest bet that I stubbornly brought to him, either.)

(But there's only so much I'll agree to.)

MC: "...I promise that I will not leave Azuchi to go with you as you march to war."

Nobunaga: "Ah, meaning you intend to follow after?"

Part 5

Nobunaga: "Ah, meaning you intend to follow after?"

Nobunaga smiled confidently.

(Normally, I love that smile of his; right now, I hated it.)

It didn't ease my distress.

MC: "...Just do one thing for me?"

MC: "Come back safe."

I placed my hand on his heart.

Nobunaga: "Who do you think I am?"

He showed no fear or confusion as before.

(Is he telling me I can trust him?)

In the end, I wasn't able to learn what secret thoughts he kept in his heart.

So all I could do was pray for him.

Several days later.

Their preparations complete, the Oda forces marched to battle against the Mouri's alliance.

......

MC: "Oh boy..."

(He really left me here...)

MC: "Excuse me. I don't suppose there's been any updates on the battle?"

Bodyguard: "...Updates?"

Bodyguard: "Sorry. We've only heard that the warlords who aligned themselves with the Mouri are also on the march."

I spoke to the guard outside my room.

They weren't there to keep me IN my room. Just in the castle. They followed me whenever I left though.

(Nobunaga was serious about me not coming with him. Gotta hand it to him.)

(...Except that no, I don't!)

At first, I was sad about him leaving. Now, I was growing increasingly angry at him!

(Putting me under all this protection just makes me want to escape!)

MC: "Soo, what are you doing tonight--?"

Bodyguard: "My lady?"

MC: "That came out wrong. When do you go on break? Like, how long does your shift last?"

Bodyguard: "Lord Nobunaga ordered you be protected all day long, so I'll be here until night."

Bodyguard: "When the night shift arrives to replace me, I'll be resting for the next shift."

(I'm never not under guard. Wonderful.)

Angry and depressed. Just then--

I heard a weird sound in the ceiling.

(Hmm? The ceiling...!)

I looked up, only to see--

MC: "Sa--!"

Sasuke: "Shh!"

(Shoot!)

Bodyguard: "Is something the matter?"

MC: "...S-sat on a pin while I thought I might get some sewing done! Silly me!"

Bodyguard: "Are you injured? Should I call someone to check on you?"

MC: "No, no, no! It didn't even hurt! I'm good!"

MC: "...But as you noticed, I like to talk to myself when I'm sewing, so don't think too much about anything you hear, okay?"

Bodyguard: "Understood, my lady."

Surprisingly, that seemed to work. Sasuke soundlessly dropped to the floor.

We talked as quietly as possible.

MC: "So, what are you doing here, 'pin'?"

Sasuke: "...There was something I had to tell you."

(Oh... Sasuke, you don't look too good.)

MC: "Are you experiencing any side effects from the recent changes to history?"

Sasuke: "...Unfortunately, yes. That's why I've been furloughed for this upcoming battle."

(I worried as much.)

Sasuke: "I've heard it's the same for you?"

MC: "Heard that from who?"

Sasuke: "That's what I came to talk to you about."

Sasuke: "You see, Nobunaga Oda came to see me while you were unconscious."

MC: "Aha!"

(That's what happened? He met with Sasuke while I was out?)

MC: "What did you two talk about?"

Sasuke: "We... that is, I--"

Sasuke: "...I told him about the future."

Sasuke: "As many details about history following the Honno-ji incident as I could."

MC: "You gave him spoilers?!"
