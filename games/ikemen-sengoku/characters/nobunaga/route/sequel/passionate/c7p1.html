Nobunaga - Sequel - Passionate - Chapter 7 (1-5)

Part 1

???: "...wake..."

(Who is it?)

???: "MC..."

(Is that you, Nobunaga? Oh, thank goodness. You woke up!)

(I was so worried that you'd--)

???: "MC? Please wake up."

I jolted up only to see Mitsunari peering at me worriedly.

MC: "Mitsunari? Where's--?"

I glanced at the tent flap. Sunlight was shining through.

(It's morning already.)

(Right... I passed out...)

Mitsunari: "I would have let you rest, but you were moaning n your sleep and I was worried."

Mitsunari: "Are you... all right?"

MC: "I'm okay. Thanks for checking on me. Anything happen while I was sleeping?"

Mitsunari: "Only that Lord Hideyoshi was asking everyone to gather for some reason."

MC: "Got it. I'll be there too."

I followed Mitsunari outside.

Last night, at sunset, camp had been full of rousing cheers of victory and a celebratory atmosphere. That was gone now.

(Something up? Or rather--)

The Oda forces were all gathered.

The familiar warlords were there too.

Standing at their head was... Hideyoshi.

(...Nothing's changed from last night. Nobunaga must still be unconscious.)

However, I felt this awful unease I couldnn't explain, like being stuck with cold needles.

Hideyoshi let out a long breath, as if savoring the brief moments before he had to speak.

Hideyoshi: "...I didn't want to have to give you this news after yesterday's victory. I didn't ever want to have to give this news."

Hideyoshi: "I think I must have spent the hours before dawn just praying that this would fall to anyone but me."

Hideyoshi: "But--"

Hideyoshi: "...It's always a possibility when someone is injured. And I was the last at his side."

("The last at his side?")

Frenetic murmurs passed through the crowd. To me, it was all noise.

(What's Hideyoshi... what's he... saying?)

Hideyoshi: "Lord Nobunaga has passed away."

("Lord Nobunaga has p...")

Keiji: "Wha--?!"

Mitsuhide: "Mm."

Ieyasu: "...Gh!"

Masamune: "...What?"

(C-could he say that again? No. No, don't. I don't... I don't want to hear what I think I heard...)

I felt sick. My world was grinding to a halt, jerking and screeching all the while and me, there, helplessly along for the ride.

Everyone else was moving through this-- this disaster in slow motion. All was silent.

Silent but for my thoughts. I repeated the news a hundred thousand times in the space it took someone to speak.

Oda Soldier 1: "That can't be!"

Someone's shout shattered the silence.

Oda Soldier 2: "Right? L-Lord Hideyoshi's too calm. So it can't be true."

Oda Soldier 3: "Meaning... it's just a joke? That's it? C'mon, don't scare us like that!"

Oda Soldier 4: "This is part of our next plan, is it? We gotta pretend that our lord is really--"

A shaky, dry laughter spread amongst a couple of the men. No one believed it. No one wanted to.

I didn't want to.

(Such a mean joke, Hideyoshi. Why would you lie and tell me--)

Tell me I'd never look into those eyes again.

Tell me I'd never see that proud smile again.

Tell me I'd never hear that deep voice again.

Tell me that that beautiful heart encased in layers of cold ice...

...had stopped beating before I had a chance to fully free it...

Should heaven and earth and the oceans risen up to swallow him, I should have split them apart to keep him near...!

Hideyoshi: "That's enough damned laughter!!"

It was so sudden I jumped at the sound.

Hideyoshi's roar froze us all in stillness.

Hideyoshi: "You don't want to believe it? Well guess what?! I don't want to believe it either!"

Hideyoshi: "You think I'd stand here in front of you all and joke about something like this?! Do you have any idea what this feels like?!"

Part 2

Hideyoshi: "You think I'd stand here in front of you all and joke about something like this?! Do you have any idea what this feels like?!"

The calm around him had been shattered. Not a man believed he was kidding when they heard how his voice quaked.

Hideyoshi: "...Our next battle is to avenge him."

Quiet and cool as his next words were, they melted the ice around everyone.

And beneath that ice was a boiling cauldron, spewing out billowing steam.

(...We're doing... what?)

Hideyoshi: "On his deathbed, our lord named the one responsible for his fall: Kicho!"

Hideyoshi: "We will head out to destroy the masterming behind his assassination!"

Hideyoshi: "It won't be easy. Our enemies lie in wait for us. But Lord Nobunaga will never rest until we see this done!"

Hideyoshi: "I know you're sad. I know you're angry. Channel those emotions into your fighting spirit! Now's the time to rise!"

A great chorus of roaring voices shook the earth. Yet I remained still.

(...I don't... I don't get it.)

How could they even find their voices? Where I was, there was nothing. No light. Not even sound.

(Channel your sadness? Channel your anger? ...What am I supposed to channel if I don't feel... anything?)

Everyone started moving. Preparing for the next battle.

(They move like men possessed...)

I watched them, their expressions livid. Every mustcle in their bodies put to the task of avenging their lord.

I felt like a ghost. Detached from reality.

(Maybe... maybe I just don't want to face reality though...)

Keiji: "Hey."

MC: "...Keiji? What's wrong?"

Keiji: "What's wrong with you is more like it."

Keiji: "You didn't jump on Hideyoshi with questions. I figured you'd be demanding to see Nobunaga's... you know, him right now."

MC: "...Mm..."

(Yeah.)

(I want to see him. I want to...)

I turned my head in the direction of his tent. I very politely pushed myself forward, but my legs very politely refused to move.

(...I don't want to.)

I knew why.

(If I go see him, then I'll know.)

(I mean, I trust Hideyoshi, so the truth is undeniable. But still--)

(I want to believe he's still alive...)

......

I found solace in my memories. Those happy months we spent together. So few of them, in retrospect.

This memory was just before Azuchi Castle had been attacked. I was preparing for a long trip to acquire some more fabric.

(I had leaving Azuchi, even if it's only for a few days. Shame we don't have cars. I'd be back later today.)

I'd woken up early and was looking around the tenshu, taking a mental picture of it to keep with me while I was gone--

Nobunaga: "...You are not to leave our bed without my permission."

MC: "Wha--!"

Strong arms wrapped around me from behind and held me snugly.

MC: "I wasn't going to leave without saying goodbye! I was letting you sleep."

As I lay my head against his chest, Nobunaga leg his own head rest atop mine.

Nobunaga: "That changes nothing."

Nobunaga: "Even if you are in my arms when I awake, you have still left them while I sleep."

Nobunaga: "If you must leave, then you may wake me to ask for permission."

Part 3

Nobunaga: "If you must leave, then you may wake me to ask for permission."

(Oh, my love. What would your enemies say if they knew the big bad Devil King was actually a BIG BABY...!)

I couldn't help but laugh at how silly Nobunaga was being right now.

MC: "Tell me why I need your permission--and make it good--or I refuse."

Nobunaga: "You wish to know why?"

Nobunaga: "It is simple. Because I have discovered how inefficient sleeping alone is."

Nobunaga: "I sleep too lightly when you are not with me. It is not restful and I become more exhausted throughout the day."

MC: "Okay. And--?"

Nobunaga: "You demand more?"

MC: "Give me something other than inefficiency. If you can't... it's no deal."

(I know I'm pushing him today. He has such trouble putting his feelings into words.)

But when I challenged him to search for it, by gosh, he seemed to be doing it.

He spent a few moments in though, as if he were literally digging for the right words.

Nobunaga: "...When I wake up... to see you aren't there... I..."

Nobunaga: "I feel... a strange chill inside me..."

(So, to him, loneliness equals cold.)

I tucked that information away for later.

  • "I'll be better."
  • "No getting cold."
  • "Okay then."

MC: "I'm satisfied. Okay then, it's a deal."

Nobunaga: "...Good."

Nobunaga smiled with relief and planted a sweet kiss on the back of my neck.

MC: "Hee! Still, it's sad to hear that sleeping alone has become so inefficient now."

MC: "Will you be all right when I'm not here?"

Nobunaga: "Hmph."

Nobunaga: "You are trying to force me to admit that I am helpless without you, is that it?"

MC: "Force?! Never! ...Well, I might take it as a compliment."

Nobunaga: "As I suspected."

MC: "No, you've got me all wrong!"

Nobunaga: "Then what was that smug look on your face just now?"

MC: "That was--"

(...That was me being smug. Yep.)

MC: "You got me."

MC: "Honestly, it makes me feel good to know that you both love and need me."

I started to blush at the wide-eyed look of surprise he gave me.

Then he gently placed his hand upon my breast, where my heart lay.

Nobunaga: "Do not feel so self-conscious about that."

Nobunaga: "It is true that there is a part of me so greatly affected by your presence that I lose all perspective without you."

Nobunaga: "But you should be proud of that, whether I am with you or alone."

Nobunaga: "For you possess what no one else has. You possess the life of Nobunaga Oda."

Nobunaga: "...Until the day your own heart stops."

......

(He can't have died...)

(I mean, my heart still beats, doesn't it?)

...Barely. But maybe barely was enough? I forced myself to begin walking.

Keiji: "MC?"

Part 4

Keiji: "MC? That's the wrong way."

MC: "I'm not going to see him yet."

Keiji: "You don't wanna see Nobunaga...?"

MC: "Because my heart hasn't given up on the hope that he's still alive."

MC: "If I see his... if I see him and see that it was just foolish hope, I..."

I couldn't even finish speaking the thought.

Even thinking about what I left unsaid was enough to tear me in two.

MC: "...Anyway, if I see him like this, I don't think I'd last the week."

MC: "I think I'd just die on the spot..."

Keiji: "...MC..."

MC: "So I can't see him..."

My voice was quaking.

(I feel like I might cry. Except that no tears are coming to my eyes.)

Keiji: "...I gotcha."

Keiji: "What comes next then?"

MC: "Next?"

Keiji: "I mean, are you gonna stay here? Go back with an escort to Azuchi? Or..."

Keiji: "...Are you gonna go with us to get revenge?"

(Me? Get revenge...?)

It went against all my morals. But here, at my weakest, it had a strange allure I'd never felt before.

MC: "What are you going to do?"

Keiji: "I'm going to Sakai. I've gotta do something with this anger, and I figure kicking the crap out of Kicho's a good start."

(Kicho...)

......

Kicho: "...Because you are the spark that keeps the flames of war burning."

Kicho: "As long as you remain alive, the world you are seeking will never come to pass."

Kicho: "Thus, in punishment for your crimes, you will spend your life doing just that."

Kicho: "You will live and keep this country engulfed in war."

......

(He told Nobunaga he would "live and keep this country engulfed in war".)

Thinking of Kicho finally brought something resembling an emotion to the surface.

(He blamed Nobunaga for causing wars, when he was the one who brought this huge mess about in the first place...)

(...Nobunaga wouldn't have even BEEN here if it weren't for him!)

...That emotion was rage.

Hideyoshi's Voice: "Careful with that!"

Startled by the sound, I looked over to see what Hideyoshi was shouting about.

MC: "What are they--? ...!"

Black armor. Nobunaga's black armor, being carried out of his tent.

It had the vague form of a person, but in the end, it was just an empty shell.

(...I... I can't...)

Seeing it was like coming face-to-face with the knowledge that he was really gone.

My world was being torn apart by loss of everything I loved in the world.

(I can't face that he's not here.)

(...I can't go back to Azuchi...)

(I don't want to see our home and know that he'll never walk those halls again.)

MC: "I'll go to Sakai too."

(If it's between embracing my rage or embracing the void, I chose rage.)

(...It's pretty much all I've got left..)

Keiji looked at me, worried.

Part 5

MC: "I'll go to Sakai too."

Keiji looked at me, worried.

MC: "Not on my own, of course. I'll go with you guys, if you'll have me along."

Keiji: "Hideyoshi's the one you'll want to be talking to, not me."

Keiji: "But I'll go with you."

Keiji escorted me over to Hideyoshi; despite his busy new station, he agreed to spare some time for us.

......

Hideyoshi: "Absolutely not."

I'd barely finished when he shot me down.

Hideyoshi: "We have no idea what Kicho's planning. I can't take you into a battle with unknown dangers."

MC: "All battles have unknown dangers. I know the risks. Please, let me go."

Hideyoshi: "...MC, be reasonable."

MC: "I'm only asking you the same."

MC: "Hideyoshi, you have to know... I can't go back to Azuchi in this state..."

Hideyoshi: "MC..."

MC: "I know I'm chasing a phantom but it's the only thing I've got holding me together."

Hideyoshi: "I don't want to be mean, but--"

Hideyoshi: "...Do you think putting you in danger is what Lord Nobunaga would have wanted?"

MC: "Maybe not. But if I was talking to him right now? I wouldn't back down."

MC: "Not until he told me, 'You're as fesity as ever. Very well, I allow it.' Then he'd smile."

Hideyoshi: "...MC, don't put me in this position."

MC: "You know I need to go. Please."

I fixed my eyes on him, hoping to convey just how important this was to me.

Hideyoshi frowned. He turned away from me, but at that moment--

Keiji: "I'm asking you too, Hideyoshi."

Keiji spoke up for me.

Hideyoshi: "You too, Keiji?"

Keiji: "I'll guard her."

Keiji: "You won't have any worries that way."

(Thanks for the support, Keiji.)

I was amazed that Keiji, in this difficult time, still had the energy to devote to others.

(I can barely keep myself together.)

They weren't discussing the sense of it like Hideyoshi and I had.

Whatever their argument for my going or not was happening in those silent gazes.

But [Keiji's] seemed to work, because after a few seconds, Hideyoshi sighed.

Hideyoshi: "All right. You can come along."

Hideyoshi: "MC, I want you to be with either Keiji or me at ALL times. Got it?"

MC: "Got it. And thank you."

I bowed to him, then to Keiji.

MC: "Thank you too, Keiji."

Keiji: "Nah. Was nothing."

Keiji: "Just something I wanted to do."

Hideyoshi: "We depart immediately and will assault Kicho's merchant house with the dawn."

Hideyoshi: "You two will accompany my division."

Keiji: "You got it, boss."

MC: "...Sounds great."

......

We left the battlefield and rode all through the night.

I was happy we didn't have any breaks.

As long as I was focused on keeping up with everyone, I didn't have to think about anything else.

......

(That's Kicho's merchant house? ...A bit more than a "house", if you ask me.)

Oda soldiers blocked off every road leading to and away from it.

The people of Sakai ran inside, shut their doors, and extinguished their lights as we entered the city.

Almost as if they knew what was coming.

Masamune: "I thought he'd have this place packed full of mercenaries in preparation for us."

Ieyasu: "Instead it's quiet. Too quiet."

Keiji: "Hey, uh, what're the chances he's not here?"

Mitsunari: "Our spies said that Kicho was seen entering and exiting his manor as normal, during and after the battle."

Masamune: "Don't suppose his strategy is to wait here politely and get crushed?"

Ieyasu: "If it is, then that's what he'll get."

(Everyone's ready to tear Kicho limb from limb... Good.)

Everyone's animosity, everyone's muderous rage choked the air.

I'd been hanging by a thread, so it wasn't like I'd been watching the others--

...But I saw now how Nobunaga's loss had shaken even his best warlords to their foundations.

Hideyoshi: "Ieyasu, don't let your emotions get the best of you."

Ieyasu: "...Uh huh. Says the guy holding his sword so tightly I can hear it rattling."

Keiji: "Too bad all the tea houses closed when we came marching in, cause I think you two could use a drink."

Even Keiji, with his jokes, had his gaze fixed sharply on Kicho's manor.

Mitsunari: "Lord Hideyoshi? Where is Lord Mitsuhide? He seems to have disappeared sometime during our march."

Hideyoshi: "Mitsuhide's fine. He took a few men to look for any hidden escape routes outside the manor."

Oda Soldier: "Lord Hideyoshi, we have the building surrounded."

Hideyoshi: "Thanks for the update."

Hideyoshi took another scouring look at the manor before addressing the soldiers.

Hideyoshi: "We'll storm in through the front. Our only objective is Kicho. Today's the day--"

Hideyoshi: "...We get our revenge."

Hideyoshi: "Everyone ready?"

(...We'd better be.)

(Ugh. I don't relish seeing Kicho again. Just thinking about him makes me tremble.)

Where my feelings would lead me should I see him again... I did not know.

(All I can do is follow my heart.)

(...Until it stops beating.)
